How to Use This To-Do List

Not every task on your list is equally important. Start by marking one or two items as "must do today." These are the tasks that move your goals forward or prevent problems later. Everything else is "nice to get done" if you have time or energy left.

You can also group tasks by energy level. Save simple, low-energy tasks like tidying or sending quick emails for when you are tired. Use your best hours of the day for deeper work like studying, writing, or planning. This way your to-do list supports your energy instead of fighting against it.

When you finish a task, take a moment to notice that win before jumping to the next thing. Small wins add up, and seeing progress is one of the best ways to stay motivated during a busy week.
